Tell the auditor about the account

CBN rules differ by account type — for example, maintenance fees are only lawful on current accounts, while card maintenance fees are only lawful on savings accounts. Getting this right is what lets the audit be exact.

Beta tester checklist

For the cleanest audit, export CSV/Excel from internet banking first. If you only have PDF, use a text-based statement rather than a scanned image. After upload, confirm Date, Narration, Debit/Credit/Amount and Balance columns before running the audit.

  • If the balance checksum passes: run the audit and export the report.
  • If the read looks wrong: adjust the header row or column roles.
  • If it still fails: download the anonymized parser diagnostic on the next screen and send only that JSON file to the beta team.

Refund demand letter

Pre-filled from your audit findings with the exact CBN citations. Fill in the bracketed details, print it on paper or send by email to your branch. If the bank does not resolve it within 14 days, escalate to the CBN Consumer Protection Department — cpd@cbn.gov.ng.

How accuracy is protected: every rule in this auditor is encoded from the CBN Guide to Charges by Banks, Other Financial and Non-Bank Financial Institutions (effective 1 January 2020), the CBN ATM Fee Circular of 10 February 2025, the Finance Act 2020 (EMTL) and the Nigeria Tax Act 2025, and is applied by date — each transaction is judged by the rule in force on its day. A violation is only declared when the charge exceeds the cap under the most generous lawful reading; anything uncertain is marked “needs review”, never guessed.
